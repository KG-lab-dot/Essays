Discipline

Can we relook at the concept of discipline?

Life
Discipline
Thought

Disciple. One who devotes themselves to a cause. One who is unwavering, who is compelled by nothing but the devotion itself. One who imitates the master. One who is truly sincere. The disciple takes account of all costs and forges ahead regardless. Will it be easy? No. But will it worth it? Does it even matter? The disciple doesn’t concern himself or herself with the outcome. What he knows innately and to be true without any doubt is that he or she cannot never walk the path laid before him. Staying in the place where they currently are is the one true risk. He is in survival mode. Fight or flight. There is not question. Only the answer. This is the beginning of a life that, whether well-know or not, will be fully lived.

To be disciplined is to be a disciple. To be disciplined is not to be mentally strong. It is not to have routines. To be disciplined doesn’t require following 7 habits of highly successful individuals. To be disciplined is to be devoted. It is to be totally encapsulated by the thing to the point where you and the thing become one. To be disciplined is to be in love. It is to be in love and sincere and fully devoted to a cause to the point where you lose yourself and become the cause. You become, it. Discipline cannot be taught. It can only be awakened. Where there is true discipline force is not required. Tensioned is released and all that is heavy becomes light. This is the place where all the worlds magic is born from.

We all arrive on the earth with gifts. Those who are gifted are simply those who have something to share. The gift is not for them to keep. It is for them to give to the world. It is not for the world to take, but for the man and woman to gift it to the world. One who is with gift will often want to gift it to the world. The reward is not the adoration from the world, or the tokens and trinkets given to him for his gifts. The reward is the giving of the gift itself. One with gifts feels the most fulfilled when he is given the opportunity to share his gifts with the world.

Interests and inclinations are anchored on the gifts that God brought you to this earth with. Be yourself. Be yourself within the context of the world. You already are everything that you want to be. Man is. This then reveals that man should never become anything either than himself. If one’s goals and aspirations fall outside of what they are, feeling lost is often the results of such pursuits. Pursuits that are implanted in our minds by parents, teachers, friends, spouses, media, and the rest of the barrage. One should anchor their pursuits to the expression of the gifts that they came to the earth with.

Interest is appreciation. It is expansive. One’s interests never feels like a prison, nor a burden, nor a chore. These feelings are all contractive and most often come from having to do things which you are not interested in doing. Appreciation comes from one’s own inner qualities. These inner qualities find expression by connecting to certain things that are found externally. For one to find their true talents and passion, they need only examine the things that they find most interesting. The things they appreciate the most. One is automatically drawn to the things they find most interesting without the need for any external motivation or coercion to do so. This is where true discipline arises. You need not convince somebody to do something they want to do. They will simply find the motivation internally, which is driven and directed by the compass of their interests.

Joy creates life. Nature is rigged to reward that which it requires the most with joy. Natures biggest priority in so far as human beings are concerned, is pro-creation. The procreative act is undoubtedly the most joyful and pleasurable act known to mankind. This act is a creative act as it gives birth to life. It creates life. We can take this simple fact and relate it to any other acts of creation. The creation needs not to be specific, as this principle applies to all that is created. One creates things that are most beautiful when one’s heart is totally happy to do so. When one would rather do nothing else. It is against the principles of nature to that which you do not want to do and expect anything great out of it. One’s heart cannot be fully invested in an activity one dreads. Thus, discipline simply arises out of an individual who is doing what they enjoy. They will simply prioritize it above all as human beings are wired to pursue that which feels good to them.

Nature is God. We are nature and nature is also us. Going against ourselves is going against nature and thus going against God. Having a true love for God is to fear going against God as nothing but catastrophe results in such a pursuit. God has naturally given every human being gifts. These gifts are one’s inclinations. The path of least resistance with regards to greatness is the path which one naturally follows.
